Challenges

The biggest issue Safra was facing was to make their clients use the online platform that the clients could periodically access and analyze all data related to the ATM transactions.


The main problems were:

  • Interface not attractive
  • Homogenous Screens
  • Tedious HTML tables with with lots of rows and columns full of numbers and statistics
  • High Bounce rate and low total visits rate

Process

I interviewed customers and Safra’s employees to design some prototypes improving the platform navigation. For starters I designed a Dashboard where the user could personalize which panels they wanted to show, like a shortcut, summarized content to the customer’s most viewed data. I used lots of graphics within these panels, so the information was right there, straight and clear.

In some screens, I was allowed to innovate splitting the customer's purchase in up to 12 installments, so one of the components was a very visual timeline detailing date of purchase, how many installments were left, price for each one, if the customer had paid off before deadline, all with intuitive colors and clear information.

The other one was a calendar showing pieces of information about the ATM, purchases, installments, type of purchase, card brands. I also developed a responsive version for it.

After concluding the project, I was set to design a prototype for the yet nonexistent app version.
